Lost income/laid-off

Employment Insurance (EI)

Up to $573 per week, up to a max of 45 weeks.

You will need 420 to 700 hours of insurable employment. If you don't qualify, you might qualify for the Canada Emergency Response Benefit (explained below).

Apply here

Canada Emergency Response Benefit

Source: Govt of Canada

$2000 per month, max four months

Eligibility:
  • workers who must stop working due to COVID19 and do not have access to paid leave or other income support.
  • workers who are sick, quarantined, or taking care of someone who is sick with COVID-19.
  • working parents who must stay home without pay to care for children that are sick or need additional care because of school and daycare closures.
  • workers who still have their employment but are not being paid because there is currently not sufficient work and their employer has asked them not to come to work.
  • wage earners and self-employed individuals, including contract workers, who would not otherwise be eligible for Employment Insurance.
No application process yet.

B.C. Emergency Benefit for Workers

Source: BC Govt

One time $1,000 benefit.

B.C. residents who receive federal Employment Insurance, or the new federal Canada Emergency Response Benefit are eligible

No application process yet.

GST credit

Source: Govt of Canada

Average of $400 per individual and $600 per couple, if eligible. Paid in May as part of annual GSTC payment amounts for the 2019-20 benefit year.

Renters

$500 rent rebate

Source: BC Housing

$500 for three months to low/moderate income households. Accepting applications in mid-April.

No evictions, freezing rents

Source: CBC

B.C. is suspending evictions and freezing rent increases in an effort to help tenants make ends meet and keep their homes for the duration of the provincial COVID-19 emergency.

Recurring bills

BC Hydro

Source: BC Govt

Customers can defer bill payments or arrange for flexible payment plans with no penalty through the COVID-19 Customer Assistance Program

Customers experiencing job loss, illness or lost wages due to COVID-19 can access grants up to $600 to pay their hydro bills through the Customer Crisis Fund

Mortgage, car payments

Source: Global News

Deferrals on a case-to-case basis. Contact your lender.

"Typically they will take the interest payment and spread it out over future payments or perhaps even the life of the mortgage," Rob Tétrault with Canaccord Genuity Wealth Management from Global News article.

ICBC car insurance

Source: ICBC

Defer payments for up to 90 days with no penalty.

If you are approaching your deferral payment due date, and you do not want the payment withdrawn on that date, call ICBC at 1-800-663-3051 to discuss payment options.

Apply online here.

Quarantined

Employment Insurance - Sick Benefit (EI)

Source: Govt of Canada

Up to $573 per week, up to a max of 15 weeks.

You must be quarantined or sick due to COVID-19, lost 40% of your pay for at least one week and worked a minimum of 600 hours.

Difference from standard sickness benefits: one-week waiting period waived; no medical certificate needed during quarantine

Select "medical leave" as reason for not working.

Special toll-free number for waiver of waiting period: 1-833-381-2725. Hours are 8:30 a.m. to 4:30 p.m.

Apply online before contacting the number.

What if I finish my quarantine, but my workplace is closed by then?

Request a layoff notice from work once your quarantine ends. Then call the EI toll-free number at 1-833-381-2725 to change your EI claim.

Student loans

Student loan repayments paused

Source: Govt of Canada

Federal government will pause the repayment of Canada Student Loans and Canada Apprentice Loans until Sept. 30, 2020, with no accrual of interest.
